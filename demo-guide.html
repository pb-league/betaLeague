Leagues, Tournaments & How to Log In

League Manager supports several types of play formats. Each league you see in the dropdown is configured independently by its admin.

Format

🔄 Round-Robin (singles or doubles)

Players rotate through courts and partners each session. The app auto-generates balanced matchups so everyone plays a roughly equal number of games and with a variety of partners. admin can tune what factors are considered to make fair matches, including times played together or against for that session or the entire league, balanced games based on average rankings, or favoring matches with all players of similar rank.

Format

👫 Queue-based court assignments (like open-play)

Players go into a queue and are assigned courts as they open up. Configure how many times winners get to keep playing. Track each individual's wins, losses, and standings across the whole season — ideal for leagues with players who may come and go.

Format

👫 Fixed Pairs Doubles

Teams are locked as permanent pairs. Match scheduling tracks team wins, losses, and standings across the whole season — ideal for a doubles league for couples.

Format

🏆 Tournament

Single-event bracket or round-robin seeding then final tournament round. Players register as participants with in-app option to collect fees, and the admin manages bracket progression. Great for charity events or one-day competitions.

Supports single-elimination, double-elimination, or swiss-style re-seeding each round.

Format

🎭 Mixed Doubles

Gender-based pairing for mixed doubles play. Players register with a gender group preference and the scheduler creates balanced male/female partner pairings per session.

Format

🏆 Ladder-League

Climb in the standings by beating harder opponents or placing in the weekly win % standings. Players can issue challenges to other players.

How to Log In

Follow these steps every time you open the app.

  1. Select your league — Choose from the dropdown list. You can also search by organization ID or league name if your league isn't visible, or enter a league ID directly. For demo all leagues are visible, but could be assigned a customerID so they are only seen if that ID is provided.
  2. Pick your player name — The roster for that league loads automatically. Select a name from the list.
  3. Enter the player's password — Each player sets a password at registration or is assigned one if the admin fills in the roster. If you forget it and have an email on file, use the "Email my password" recovery link. Players may change their password.
  4.  — Players land on the player dashboard. Admins who choose Admin Login reach the admin panel.

Things to Try Out

Once logged in, here are the most interesting flows to explore — split by player and admin experience.

🧑‍🤝‍🧑 As a Player

Player

View Your Schedule

See your upcoming and past match assignments — court, opponent/partner, and time — all in one place on your dashboard.

Player

Check the Standings

Browse the live leaderboard for your league — wins, losses, points, and ranking updated in real time as scores are entered.

Can check the overall standings, or standings for each session.

If the leage is a ladder league, also view the ladder standings and details of how they were determined.

Player

Update Your Profile

Change your display name, update your email for score notifications, or upload a profile photo directly from the app.

Player

View your Player Report

Shows your record playing with and against each other player, all your matches, and if they were upsets based on rankings of players.

Player

Mark Availability

If your league uses availability tracking, flag sessions you can or can't attend so the admin can account for absences when generating schedules.

🔐 As an Admin

Admin

Modify League Settings

Add a new play session with date, number of courts, and duration. The system is now ready to generate matchups for it.

Admin

Generate Match Schedule

After adjusting the weightings for the various factors, Hit the schedule generator for a session — watch the app produce balanced court assignments based on the chosen factors.

Can also pre-assign some people to courts to guarantee they play in the first round.

Admin

Edit or Override a Score

Find a completed match and manually adjust the score — useful when scores were entered incorrectly or someone was unable to play. Standings update immediately.

Admin

Manage Players

Add, deactivate, or edit players. Adjust skill ratings, reset passwords, or switch someone between player and spectator roles.

Admin

League Settings

Explore the configuration panel — toggle self-registration on/off, set max participants, configure an invite code, adjust scoring rules, and manage email notifications.

Admin

Send a Broadcast Message

Use the messaging feature to notify all active players about an upcoming session, cancellation, or league update via email.

Admin

Generate an Invite Code

Create a one-time or reusable invite code players need during self-registration. Great for controlling who can join a private league.

💡
Pro tip: Try logging in as a player in one browser tab and as an admin in another. Enter a score as the player and watch the standings update — then approve or edit it as the admin. This gives you the full picture of how both sides of the app interact.

Quick Reference

🗺️ Navigation Flow

Login page → select league → select player (or Admin Login) → enter password (0000 for players, 0001 for admin on the demo leagues) → Player Dashboard or Admin Panel

📱 PWA / Mobile

The app works as a Progressive Web App. On mobile, tap Add to Home Screen from your browser menu to install it as a native-feeling app. It will auto-refresh when a new version is published. This also allows push notifications to be sent to you.

💳 Registration Fees (Stripe)

If a league has a registration fee configured, players are redirected to a Stripe payment page during sign-up. In demo mode this may be skipped or use Stripe's test card: 4242 4242 4242 4242, any future expiry, any CVC.

📧 Email Notifications

Players with a registered email address can receive session results, score confirmations, and password recovery emails. Admins can trigger broadcast emails from the admin panel. In demo mode, actual emails may be suppressed.
